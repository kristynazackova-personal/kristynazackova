# Path to $20K/month and quitting the day job

*Personal plan · April 2026*

## 1. Be honest about the number

The stated goal is "$20K/month so I can leave my day job." Before planning, that number has to be translated into the gross revenue that actually delivers $20K of useful, reliable income. As a self-employed solo operator in the US, the realistic math is:

| Item | % of gross | On $25K gross/mo |
|---|---|---|
| Federal + state income tax | ~22–28% | $6.0K |
| Self-employment tax (SS+Medicare) | ~15.3% on first ~$170K | $3.2K |
| Health insurance (family, ACA marketplace, no subsidy at this income) | — | $1.5–2.5K |
| Retirement (Solo 401k, optional but should not skip) | 10–15% | $2.5–3.5K |
| Business expenses (tools, accountant, legal) | — | $0.5–1.0K |
| **Net to lifestyle** | | **~$10–12K/mo** |

So "$20K/month" needs reframing. Two cleaner versions of the goal:

- **(A) Replace W2 take-home.** If current Principal PM total comp is ~$220–300K with benefits, the equivalent self-employed gross is ~$28–35K/month. Plan accordingly.
- **(B) Hit $20K gross/month, accept lifestyle tightening.** ~$10–12K/mo to live on. Workable but tight in any major US metro.

**The plan below targets $25K gross/month sustained for 3 consecutive months as the trigger to quit.** That gives ~$12–15K of usable income, leaves ~$5–7K margin if a client churns, and matches the W2 floor closely enough.

## 2. Be honest about what gets you there

ASAP and "from products" are mutually exclusive. ConversationLens at consumer mobile pricing ($2–5/wk) needs **5,000–10,000 paying subscribers** to clear $20K MRR. Going from "active users, low thousands" to that number realistically takes 12–24 months even with a great execution streak.

The fastest legal path to $20K/month for someone with a Principal PM track record + AI-builder credibility + a live shipped product is **services**, specifically:

1. **Fractional product leadership** at $250–400/hr or $7–12K/mo retainers.
2. **Productized AI-product consulting** (audits, sprints, strategy intensives) at $5–15K flat.
3. **Mentorship saturation** at MentorCruise + direct.
4. **Product MRR** as the *replacement* income stream, not the bridge.

The plan is therefore two-phase:

- **Phase 1 (months 0–6):** Stack services + mentorship + slow product MRR growth to hit $25K gross sustained. Quit.
- **Phase 2 (months 6–24):** Trade services hours back for product MRR until products carry it.

Phase 1 is what gets you out of the day job. Phase 2 is what makes the freedom real.

## 3. Phase 1 — the bridge to $25K/month sustained

### Revenue stack target (by month 6)

| Stream | Monthly target | What it takes |
|---|---|---|
| Fractional PM retainers (2 clients @ $8K) | $16K | 30–40 hrs/week total |
| Productized engagements (1/quarter avg) | $3K | 1 audit/sprint per quarter, amortized |
| MentorCruise + direct mentees | $3K | 8–12 active mentees |
| ConversationLens MRR | $2K | ~600 paid subs, modest growth from current |
| ThreadLift MRR | $1K | ~10–15 paid customers |
| **Total** | **$25K** | |

Note this stack does **not** rely on any product hitting product-market-fit miracles. Every line is achievable from a current state of "credentialed PM with shipped products." If any product line over-delivers, that's upside, not the plan.

### Month-by-month execution

**Month 0 (this month) — Setup.**

- Form a single-member LLC (or use existing). Open a business bank account. Hire an accountant on retainer ($150–300/mo) — this pays for itself the first quarter.
- Decide W2 quit date: month 6 conditional on 3 consecutive months of $25K gross.
- Audit personal cash position. Need 6 months of household expenses in liquid savings before quitting, separate from business cash.
- Pick a healthcare plan path: ACA marketplace (most likely), spouse plan if applicable, or COBRA bridge for the first 18 months.
- Build the offering page. One page, on kristynazackova.com, with three packages:
  - **Fractional Product Lead** — $8K/mo, 20 hrs/week, 3-month minimum.
  - **AI Product Audit** — $7.5K flat, 2-week sprint, deliverable is a written report + roadmap.
  - **Discovery Sprint** — $12K flat, 4 weeks, ends with a validated problem/solution brief and a build plan.
- Stop everything else that isn't on this list. ThreadLift and Life Made Easy in particular get downgraded to "shipping fixes only, no new features." 3D Space gets paused.

**Month 1 — First retainer + mentorship saturation.**

- Goal: 1 fractional retainer signed ($8K MRR) + max out MentorCruise.
- Where the first retainer comes from, in priority order:
  1. **Direct outbound to your network.** 30–50 personalized LinkedIn / email messages to former colleagues, ex-clients from Inmar/Bazaarvoice, founders you've mentored. The pitch: "I'm taking on 2 fractional product clients before going full-time independent." Network warmth converts at 5–15%; that's 2–7 conversations, 1–2 deals.
  2. **Founder communities you're already in.** OnDeck, South Park Commons, Lenny's Slack, Reforge alumni, MentorCruise mentee referrals. Post the offering once in each, in a non-spammy way ("here's what I'm doing, DM if relevant").
  3. **AI-product-specific niches.** You have a public migration story (Replit → Claude Code) and a live AI product (CL). Post a thread on X/LinkedIn that ends with the offering. The Claude Code blog post is a credibility asset — link to it.
- MentorCruise: raise rate to top-quartile ($300–400/mo per mentee), accept all fitting requests until calendar caps you at ~10 mentees. Add an offer for 90-min one-shot strategy sessions at $400 — these convert mentorship-curious prospects without a subscription.
- Phase 1 product work this month: rework the CL Premium/Plus tier matrix from the previous strategy doc. This is the highest-ROI single product change and takes <2 weeks.

**Month 2 — Second retainer + first productized engagement.**

- Goal: 2 retainers signed ($16K MRR), 1 productized engagement closed ($7.5K one-time), mentorship at $3K/mo.
- The second retainer almost always comes from a referral from the first. Ask the first client at week 4 ("if you know anyone else who'd benefit from this, I'm taking one more"). Referrals close at 30–50%.
- The productized engagement comes from the LinkedIn / X post from month 1 — leads that didn't want a 3-month commitment but had a defined problem.
- Critical discipline: **do not sign a third retainer.** Two is the time-budget cap if products are going to grow. A third retainer means products freeze for the year.
- Phase 1 product work: ship the CL Couples Plan + voice credits.

**Months 3–4 — Hold the stack, prove stability.**

- No new client acquisition. Deliver well, ask for case studies and quotable testimonials, raise referral surface.
- Product work focus: CL acquisition flywheel — 4–6 SEO posts targeting high-intent queries, gifting SKU launch, therapist pilot recruitment (10 LMFTs, free in exchange for case studies).
- Cash position checkpoint at end of month 4: should be at $25K gross/mo run-rate. If not, see §5 for the pivot.

**Month 5 — Quit-readiness audit.**

- 3 consecutive months at $25K+? Yes → set quit date for end of month 6.
- 6+ months household expenses liquid, separate from business? Yes → confirm.
- Health insurance plan signed and active for the day after quit date? Yes → confirm.
- One client retainer with a renewal commitment past the quit date? Yes → confirm.
- Tell the day job. Standard notice is 2 weeks; for a Principal role, offer 4–6 weeks if it preserves the relationship — references and possible boomerang consulting income matter.

**Month 6 — Quit. Don't celebrate yet.**

The first month independent is the most dangerous month. It's the moment most people *expand expenses*, *take on a third retainer because the offers are flowing*, or *start a new product because the freedom feels great*. Don't. Hold the stack for 90 days post-quit. Then start phase 2.

## 4. Phase 2 — replace services with product MRR (months 6–24)

The bridge income is *bridge*. The actual freedom comes from products that earn while you sleep. From month 6 onward, the goal shifts:

- Hold services revenue flat at $19–22K/mo (2 retainers + mentorship + occasional productized work).
- Grow product MRR from ~$3K to ~$15K over 18 months.
- At month 24, choose: drop one retainer (services now $11K, products $15K, total still ~$26K), or keep the stack and bank the surplus.

**Where the product MRR comes from, in priority order:**

1. **ConversationLens couples + therapist channel.** Plan in `conversationlens-monetization-strategy.md` is the playbook. Realistic month-24 contribution: $8–12K MRR.
2. **ThreadLift B2B SaaS.** Slow but real if you keep it alive at low effort. $20–60/mo per customer, 50–150 customers achievable in 18 months given the founder-Twitter audience overlap. $2–5K MRR.
3. **Productized digital products from your IP.** A $99 pre-marital course derived from ConversationLens content; a $499 "AI Product Audit" template + Loom walkthrough sold to other PMs; a $19/mo "AI builder operator" newsletter once audience size justifies it. $1–3K MRR within 12 months of consistent shipping.

**The discipline that makes phase 2 work:** Tuesday/Thursday/half-of-Friday for client work. Monday/Wednesday/half-of-Friday for product. Weekends off, mostly. If clients eat product days more than once a quarter, the stack is failing — drop a retainer before the products die.

## 5. The pivot if month 4 isn't on track

If at end of month 4 you're at <$18K/mo run-rate, do not push the quit date out by trying harder on the same plan. Diagnose:

- **No retainers signed?** The offer is wrong, the price is wrong, or the channels are too narrow. Most common fix: drop the retainer minimum from 3 months to 1 month, drop the price to $5K/mo for the first month as a "trial," and double the outbound volume. Retainers usually come from boring outbound, not viral content.
- **Retainers signed but mentorship/products lagging?** This is fine. Push quit date to month 8, not month 12. Don't lose the urgency.
- **Retainers signed but eating all available time?** You've built a self-employed job, not freedom. Raise rates 30% on the next renewal and use the freed hours for product. If the client churns, replace them at the higher rate or don't replace them at all.

Default rule: do not quit without 3 consecutive months at the target. Run-rate is not income. One $30K month followed by two $12K months is not $25K/mo — it's a $17K/mo business with variance.

## 6. What to stop doing immediately

ASAP plans fail on focus, not effort. Concretely:

- **Pause 3D Space (lifeos.solutions) entirely.** It's a beta personal-exploration project with no revenue and no clear monetization. It can come back in year 3.
- **Freeze Life Made Easy in QA.** Don't ship it until phase 1 is done. Shipping a fourth product while trying to leave the day job is the single most common failure pattern for multi-venture founders.
- **Cap ThreadLift at maintenance mode.** Bug fixes, support tickets, no new features until month 12. It's the second-best product bet but it can't be a primary focus.
- **Don't take on speaking gigs, podcast tours, or content sprints unless they directly funnel into the offering page.** Audience-building is a 2026–2027 project, not a phase-1 input.
- **Don't write a book, course, or community.** All three are tempting, all three are 6-month distractions that don't pay until month 9.

The ventures portfolio is a strength on the offering page (proof you ship) and a liability on the calendar (every project taxes attention). Phase 1 is single-product (CL) + services. Period.

## 7. The honest summary

- **Fastest path to $20K/mo is services + mentorship, not products.** Anyone selling you a 90-day product-only plan to that number is selling.
- **Real target is $25K gross sustained for 3 months**, because taxes/healthcare/runway eat the difference.
- **Quit at month 6 conditional on the stack being real**, not on optimism.
- **Phase 2 is where product MRR replaces services**, slowly, over 18 months. That's where actual leverage shows up.
- **Focus is the constraint.** Pause 3D Space. Freeze Life Made Easy. Cap ThreadLift. Run CL + services + mentorship and nothing else until phase 1 closes.

The path is unromantic but it's the one that actually works. Most PMs-turned-founders fail at this exact transition not because their products are bad but because they tried to leap directly from W2 to product MRR and ran out of cash 9 months in. The bridge is the unsexy answer and it's the right one.
